# 🚀 My Learning Journey with 100 Days of SwiftUI

## 🌟 Overview

As an engineering student, I embarked on the **100 Days of SwiftUI** challenge to dive deep into Apple's modern UI framework. This journey has equipped me with the skills to build real-world iOS apps and understand the fundamentals of SwiftUI.

## 📂 Repository Structure

- **/Playgrounds:** Swift Playground files for interactive coding.
- **/Projects:** Complete Xcode projects developed throughout the course.

## 🎯 What I've Learned

### **Swift Programming:**
- Mastered the basics: variables, constants, strings, and numbers.
- Explored advanced topics: optionals, closures, and protocols.

### **SwiftUI Fundamentals:**
- Gained a strong understanding of SwiftUI components, views, and modifiers.
- Learned to build complex UIs and implement features like navigation, data binding, and animations.

### **App Development:**
- Developed multiple projects showcasing my skills.
- Adopted best practices for coding, debugging, and performance optimization.

## 🏗️ Projects I've Built

Throughout the 100 days, I completed several projects that demonstrate my ability to build complete, functional iOS applications. These projects are not just exercises but real-world apps that solve problems and provide value to users. They include:

- **Utility Apps:** Apps like a tip calculator and sleep tracker that offer practical solutions for everyday needs.
- **Games:** Interactive games that challenge users and improve engagement.
- **Advanced Features:** Integration of external frameworks like Core Image and MapKit, showcasing my ability to work with complex functionalities.

These projects have been instrumental in helping me understand the full development cycle, from ideation to deployment. They have also allowed me to explore different aspects of iOS development, making me a versatile and skilled developer.
